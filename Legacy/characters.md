Gnoll Barbarian
Female, middle aged, gnoll, exile of her clan in the Scarred Sands, Ygara was born to the Pack of the Brass Claw, a nomadic gnoll tribe of ruthless hunters. 
As a brutal and accomplished hunter, she quickly rose through the ranks and earned the eye of the alpha males of the tribe. During her time in the pack she bore and raised several children with the chieftain, Abrax. The last of them were a pair of twins, Ygram, the first born, and Gamal, the second. While Ygram was taken in to be fostered and trained, Gamal was cast aside. Gamal had been born an albino, which was seen as an ill omen, an affront to the Death God Yeenoghu. As such he was to be offered as sacrifice and culled from the tribe. In a desperate bid to save his life, Ygara took her son and stole away into the night. It took several days, but eventually she was hunted down and captured, her son was promptly taken from her, never to be seen again. She was brought before the Elders for her opposition to the clan and her Lord’s will. Her sentimentality was deemed weakness in their eyes. Such weakness jeopardized the strength of the clan, thus, she would henceforth be banished from the tribe, never to return lest she be hunted like prey.
In the seven years since her banishment, she survived through taking on mercenary work and odd jobs across the desert. Proving to still be a capable warrior and threat to the nomads, she served as bodyguard to the merchant caravans she formerly raided along the Scar. Eventually however the work dried up and she began wandering the desert as a vagrant. Scrounging whatever she could find. One day she came upon an Elven monastery where she sought refuge and food. Initially she attempted to steal away into the cellars and take her fill, though she was apprehended. The elf, Tefnare, either through pity, or fascination, decided to give her half a loaf of bread and water. They spoke only briefly and Ygara departed. To her surprise and annoyance, the elf followed. For a time after the two became travel companions and on several occasions, cell mates.

The Gnoll tribes of the Scarred Sands
There are several nomadic gnoll packs that inhabit and roam the reaches of the Scarred Sands. Rival packs compete for territory and hunting grounds, while few have found alliance with one another.
The Scarred Sands, more simply as ‘The Scar’ are a large swath of desert, notable for the deep black sand. Myth tells that this is the place where Yeenoghu, the God of Death, fought, and was slain during his battle with the God of War. It is said that the sands were permanently stained by his blood. As it left a permanent blackened scar across the very earth. Travelers and merchants are warned about venturing across The Scar lest they be accosted by the gnoll tribes or other beasts that plague the region. The jackal headed gnoll tribes roam these lands as disciples of the fallen god, preying upon those that risk the journey.
The blood of this fallen god is said to have given rise to the gnoll tribes. The embalmed corpse of the god of death is also rumored to be kept in the obsidian obelisk within the Scar. Only the death priests are allowed entrance into the sacred chambers to provide sacrifice and maintain the body. Despite their rivalry, it is understood that all gnolls, being of his blood, pay homage to Yeenoghu and respect his priests and their rites.
The priests believe that one day a proper vessel will be found through which the spirit of their god can be resurrected and live among them once more.
